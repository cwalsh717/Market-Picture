An Bradán Feasa

The Salmon of Knowledge

Bradán is the Irish word for salmon. In Irish mythology, An Bradán Feasa — the Salmon of Knowledge — swam in the Well of Wisdom, having consumed the nine hazelnuts of knowledge that fell from the surrounding hazel trees. The poet Finegas spent seven years trying to catch it. When his apprentice Fionn mac Cumhaill finally cooked the salmon, he burned his thumb on a drop of its fat and instinctively sucked it — accidentally gaining all the world's knowledge. From then on, Fionn could access that wisdom simply by biting his thumb.
